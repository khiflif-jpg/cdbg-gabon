Introduction

In Gabon, the forest covers about 88Â % of the national territory and is a strategic resource. The State owns these forest lands and strictly regulates their use to ensure sustainable and equitable management. Companies wishing to exploit timber must obtain a specific title and comply with stringent social, environmental and fiscal obligations.

Legal framework for forest exploitation

The Gabonese Forest Code stipulates that the forest belongs to the State. Two types of exploitation titles coexist:

  • CFAD (Forest Concession for Sustainable Management): a 30â€‘year renewable exploitation title based on a management plan and including social, environmental and fiscal obligations.

Concession allocation procedure

Access to a CFAD follows a rigorous procedure made up of several stages:

  1. Public call for tenders: the Ministry publishes a call for tenders specifying the available forest blocks and the selection criteria.
  2. Application dossier: the company submits a comprehensive file including a financing plan, bank guarantees, impact studies and social commitments.
  3. Provisional agreement and management plan: after analysis, the successful bidder signs a threeâ€‘year provisional agreement to draw up the management plan and consult local communities.
  4. Signature of the definitive convention: after the plan and consultations, a 30â€‘year definitive convention is signed with the State.

Local processing imperative

SinceÂ 2010, the export of unprocessed logs has been banned. Concession holders must process wood locally (sawing, peeling, plywood) in order to create value and jobs. Forest certification and traceability are gradually being incorporated as complementary requirements.

Conclusion

Access to a Gabonese forest concession is not tantamount to buying land but to obtaining a right of exploitation subject to specific obligations. The allocation procedure and the local processing requirement aim to guarantee sustainable exploitation that benefits the national economy and future generations.
