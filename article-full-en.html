Gabon strengthens its forest policy: fighting illegal logging, certification, and traceability

Published on October 6, 2025 ‚Äî Source: CDBG Magazine

üåç A leader in sustainable forest management

With around 88% of its territory covered by equatorial forests, Gabon stands as a leading African country in sustainable forest management. Guided by the Ministry of Water and Forests, the government has intensified efforts to combat illegal logging and deforestation.

üîé Stronger field monitoring

Several concrete actions have been implemented this year: increased surveillance of concessions, regular inspections, river patrols, and partnerships with specialized NGOs. These measures aim to ensure a legal timber value chain and protect biodiversity.

üìå PAFC & FSC certification and digital traceability

All forest concessions are now pursuing sustainable certification under PAFC (Pan-African Forest Certification) and FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) standards. These labels guarantee legality, traceability, and sustainability, adding value to Gabonese timber on global markets.

The National Timber Traceability System (SNTBG) provides end-to-end digital tracking from logging to export, enabling full transparency and rapid detection of irregularities.

ü§ù Partnerships with NGOs and international actors

The strategy‚Äôs success also relies on collaboration with NGOs such as Brainforest, The Nature Conservancy (TNC), and WWF Gabon. They play a key role in training, environmental monitoring, and promoting responsible practices.

üåç Global recognition and recent commitments

Gabon has hosted several international events focused on combating illegal deforestation and promoting a legal, certified timber value chain. These meetings strengthened public-private partnerships and boosted the visibility of PAFC/FSC-certified wood worldwide.

üìå Conclusion

A modern, legal, and sustainable timber industry is taking shape in Gabon. Through PAFC/FSC certification, SNTBG digital traceability, and reinforced government oversight, Gabon positions itself as a model of responsible forest management in Africa. CDBG fully supports this national policy, contributing to the sector‚Äôs credibility and international promotion.
