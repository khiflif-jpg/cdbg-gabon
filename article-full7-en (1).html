Process, costs and timelines

Certification takes place in several stages:

  1. Decision and commitment: the company commits at the highest level to obtaining certification and mobilises human and financial resources.
  2. Bringing operations into compliance: initial diagnosis, staff training and adaptation of practices to meet the requirements of the standard.
  3. Initial audit: evaluation by an independent certification body. If successful, the certificate is issued for five years.
  4. Maintaining the certificate: annual surveillance audits and continuous improvement of practices.
